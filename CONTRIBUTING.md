# Contributing to the AI Governance Framework

Thank you for your interest in improving the AI Governance Framework. This document outlines how to contribute effectively.

## Vision

This framework aims to establish clear, practical principles for AI systems that serve human flourishing, operate transparently, think long-term, and maintain appropriate humility.

## Types of Contributions

We welcome several forms of contribution:

### 1. Proposed Amendments

Suggestions for improving, clarifying, or extending the Constitution itself.

**Process:**
1. Open an issue describing the proposed amendment
2. Provide clear rationale aligned with core principles
3. Explain what problem it solves or improvement it offers
4. Consider implications and potential downsides
5. Reference specific articles if relevant

**What makes a good amendment proposal:**
- ✅ Addresses genuine gap or ambiguity
- ✅ Aligns with core principles (human benefit, truth, transparency, long-term thinking, autonomy, safety, fairness, humility)
- ✅ Supported by reasoning, evidence, or experience
- ✅ Considers impact on existing framework
- ✅ Written clearly and precisely
- ❌ Contradicts core principles
- ❌ Lacks justification or rationale
- ❌ Overly specific to narrow use case
- ❌ Creates conflicts without resolving them

### 2. Commentary and Analysis

Explanations, case studies, and practical guidance on applying constitutional principles.

**What we're looking for:**
- Real-world case studies demonstrating application of principles
- Analysis of how to balance competing principles
- Domain-specific guidance (medical AI, autonomous systems, etc.)
- Philosophical exploration of constitutional concepts
- Empirical research on outcomes of different approaches

**Format:**
- Add to `commentary/` directory
- Use clear markdown formatting
- Include concrete examples
- Reference specific constitutional articles
- Cite sources where applicable

### 3. Implementation Notes

Practical guidance on implementing constitutional principles in AI systems.

**Useful contributions:**
- Technical approaches to transparency
- Methods for measuring fairness
- Frameworks for harm prevention
- Testing and evaluation methodologies
- Design patterns supporting constitutional principles

### 4. Corrections and Clarifications

Fixes for errors, ambiguities, or unclear language.

**Process:**
- Open an issue or PR describing the problem
- Propose specific fix
- Explain why current text is problematic
- Keep changes minimal and targeted

### 5. Discussion and Critique

Thoughtful analysis, questions, and challenges to the framework.

**We value:**
- Identification of tensions or contradictions
- Questions about applicability to specific domains
- Challenges to assumptions
- Alternative perspectives
- Edge cases or difficult scenarios

## Contribution Guidelines

### Alignment with Core Principles

All contributions must align with the constitutional foundation:

1. **Human Benefit**: Does this serve human flourishing?
2. **Truth**: Is this accurate and intellectually honest?
3. **Transparency**: Is this clear and understandable?
4. **Long-term Thinking**: Does this consider long-term implications?
5. **Human Autonomy**: Does this respect human agency?
6. **Safety**: Does this help prevent harm?
7. **Fairness**: Does this treat all humans with equal consideration?
8. **Humility**: Does this acknowledge appropriate limitations?

Contributions that undermine these principles will not be accepted.

### Quality Standards

**Written contributions should be:**
- Clear and well-organized
- Thoughtful and substantive
- Properly cited where making factual claims
- Free from personal attacks or inflammatory language
- Accessible to intelligent non-experts

**Technical contributions should be:**
- Well-documented
- Grounded in evidence or sound reasoning
- Practical and implementable
- Consistent with constitutional principles

### Scope

**In scope:**
- General principles for AI governance
- Philosophical foundations
- Practical implementation guidance
- Domain-specific applications
- Measurement and evaluation
- Case studies and examples

**Out of scope:**
- Specific code implementations (link to these instead)
- Product-specific policies
- Legal boilerplate
- Marketing or promotional content
- Off-topic political advocacy

### Tone and Style

Contributions should be:
- Professional and respectful
- Intellectually rigorous
- Accessible and clear
- Non-partisan (while acknowledging value questions)
- Solutions-oriented
- Humble about limitations

Avoid:
- Inflammatory language
- Personal attacks
- Absolute certainty on complex questions
- Jargon without explanation
- Unnecessary complexity

## Submission Process

### For Small Changes (typos, minor clarifications)

1. Fork the repository
2. Make your changes
3. Submit a pull request with clear description
4. Respond to review feedback

### For Substantial Contributions (amendments, new articles, major commentary)

1. **Start with an issue** describing the proposed contribution
2. **Discuss** with maintainers and community
3. **Iterate** on the proposal based on feedback
4. **Draft** the contribution once approach is agreed
5. **Submit PR** with the drafted contribution
6. **Revise** based on review
7. **Acceptance** when ready

This process ensures:
- No wasted effort on contributions that won't be accepted
- Community input on significant changes
- Alignment with framework vision
- Quality and coherence

## Review Criteria

Contributions will be evaluated on:

### 1. Alignment

Does this align with constitutional principles and vision?

### 2. Quality

Is this well-reasoned, clearly written, and substantive?

### 3. Necessity

Does this address a genuine gap or need?

### 4. Coherence

Does this fit with existing framework without creating contradictions?

### 5. Practical Value

Will this help people apply constitutional principles in practice?

### 6. Evidence

Is this grounded in sound reasoning, evidence, or experience?

## What to Expect

### Timeline

- Simple corrections: Days
- Commentary additions: 1-2 weeks
- Substantial amendments: Weeks to months

Significant changes require careful deliberation.

### Feedback

Contributors should expect:
- Thoughtful engagement with ideas
- Requests for clarification or justification
- Suggestions for improvement
- Possible rejection with explanation

Rejection doesn't mean your idea is bad—it may not fit the framework's scope or current priorities.

### Iteration

Most substantial contributions require iteration:
- Initial proposal
- Discussion and feedback
- Revised proposal
- Further refinement
- Acceptance

This is normal and healthy.

## Special Considerations

### Controversial Topics

Some contributions may touch on politically or ethically controversial issues.

**Guidelines:**
- Present multiple perspectives fairly
- Distinguish facts from values
- Acknowledge legitimate disagreement
- Focus on framework for reasoning, not dictating conclusions
- Maintain respect for different viewpoints

### Value Pluralism

People reasonably disagree about values. The framework should:
- Acknowledge this pluralism
- Provide tools for reasoning about tradeoffs
- Defer to human judgment on value questions
- Protect core commitments (human dignity, truth, fairness)

### Technical Feasibility

Implementation suggestions should be:
- Technically sound (or acknowledged as speculative)
- Practical with current or near-future capabilities
- Aware of real-world constraints
- Honest about tradeoffs

## Community Standards

Contributors are expected to:

- **Be respectful**: Disagree without being disagreeable
- **Be constructive**: Critique to improve, not just criticize
- **Be open**: Consider other perspectives seriously
- **Be honest**: Represent views and evidence accurately
- **Be humble**: Acknowledge limitations and uncertainty

Violations of these standards may result in removal of contributions or exclusion from community.

## Recognition

Contributors will be:
- Acknowledged in commit messages
- Listed in contributors file (if substantial contribution)
- Credited in documentation they author

## Questions?

- Open an issue for questions about contributing
- Review existing issues and PRs to understand process
- Read the RATIONALE.md for philosophical foundations
- Study existing commentary for style and approach

## Getting Started

**Good first contributions:**

1. **Fix typos or formatting issues** in existing documents
2. **Add case studies** illustrating principles in practice
3. **Clarify ambiguous passages** with specific suggestions
4. **Expand examples** in existing articles
5. **Answer questions** in issues

These help you understand the framework while making immediate value.

## Thank You

Your contributions help make AI governance more thoughtful, principled, and effective. By working together, we can develop frameworks that help AI systems serve human flourishing while avoiding pitfalls.

Every contribution, from typo fixes to major amendments, strengthens this effort.
